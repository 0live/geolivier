## 🏔️ Evolution of the Ossoue Glacier (Pyrenees) since 1850

This research project aimed to reconstruct the morphological and volumetric evolution of the Ossoue Glacier since the end of the Little Ice Age. The main challenge was to accurately quantify the retreat of this ice mass, from heterogeneous data, in the face of climate change to validate its role as a "sentinel" and regional climate proxy. <a href="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/281715707_Evolution_of_Ossoue_Glacier_French_Pyrenees_since_the_end_of_the_Little_Ice_Age" target="_blank">The study reveals an alarming acceleration of melting, forecasting a probable disappearance of the glacier by the middle of the 21st century.</a>

## Technical Expertise & Geomatics

Conducting this study required setting up a complex workflow to merge and harmonize heterogeneous data covering over 160 years.

- Multi-source photogrammetry: Generation of high-precision Digital Elevation Models (DEMs) from historical ground photographs, IGN aerial photographs, and stereoscopic pairs from the Pléiades satellite.
- Spatial modeling and interpolation: Digitization of old maps (including the 1851 Staff map) and algorithm processing (ANUDEM) to create coherent topographic surfaces.
- Data collection: In-situ measurement of the glacier surface by ice coring and differential GPS (illustration photos).
- Geodetic mass balance analysis: Differential calculation by pixel-by-pixel DEM subtraction to quantify variations in ice volume and thickness.
- Radar data exploitation (GPR): Processing of ground-penetrating radar profiles to map the bedrock and model the residual ice volume.
- Statistics and climate correlations: Time series analysis to establish links between glacial retreat and global climate indices such as the NAO (North Atlantic Oscillation) and AMO.
