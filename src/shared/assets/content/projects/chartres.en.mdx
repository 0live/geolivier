## Large-scale view cones analysis

The objective was to quantify and qualify the visibilities on the Chartres Cathedral over a study area with a 300 km radius. The technical challenge lay in the massive processing of 900 million elevation cells (1m DEM) and the reconstruction of a historical state of the territory to precisely measure the impact of urbanization on landscapes.

## Technical implementation

- **Visibility Modeling**: Implementation of visibility calculations over 70,600 hectares, integrating Earth curvature and atmospheric refraction corrections to guarantee the accuracy of results over long distances.
- **Temporal Analysis**: Creation of a "1950s" Digital Surface Model (DSM) by analyzing historical aerial photographs, making it possible to quantify the visibility losses related to urban development over the last 50 years.
- **Urban Planning Support**: Mapping of the maximum authorized building heights (available heights) to ensure the non-degradation of current view cones.
- **Spatial Analysis**: Storage and processing of raster data directly in the database by combining PostGIS native spatial analysis functions.
- **Calculation Optimization**: Management of 15 days of machine time to process raw visibilities over the entire elevation dataset.
- **Visualization**: Use of CesiumJS combined with the Digital Elevation Model (DEM) to visualize the results of the visibility analysis and the 3D view cones.
