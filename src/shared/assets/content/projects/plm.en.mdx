## An unprecedented marine cadastre project

The objective of this mission was to transpose the principles of terrestrial land registry to the maritime space
in order to centralize the management of rights and uses on the coastal strip.
The institutional challenge lay in mobilizing and coordinating around forty stakeholders
with divergent interests, such as the National Navy, IFREMER, and various local authorities, to collect their data.
Technically, the major challenge was to transform a mass of scattered textual regulatory information into a coherent geographical reference system, visualizable in 3D, at a time when 3D GIS tools were not yet mature.

## Technical implementation

- **Data Engineering**: Integration and standardization of heterogeneous feeds (Open Data, WFS feeds, flat files) from around forty national and local partners.
- **ETL Automation**: Setup of automated processing pipelines with FME for converting regulatory data into geographical objects and generating underwater 3D data.
- **WebGIS Development**: Creation of a platform with an administration interface, relying on ExtJS, a reference framework at the time, and the Google Earth API for 3D visualization.
- **Backend Architecture**: Stack built around PHP for business logic, PostGIS for complex spatial storage, and MapServer for broadcasting cartographic feeds to OGC standards.
