## A single point of access for geographic data

Developed for the GIS Centre of Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF), the <a href="https://geo.msf.org/home" target="_blank">geoplatform</a> serves as a one-stop shop for accessing all of the organization's geographic resources. The main objective is to support teams, both at headquarters and in the field, in their operational decision-making through tools adapted to humanitarian challenges. Another key goal was to provide a platform accessible enough to raise awareness among non-technical users about the added value of mapping in their daily operations.

The major challenge was to centralize and structure a vast amount of data produced daily for various international missions, while ensuring optimal accessibility for different types of users (logisticians, program managers, cartographers, etc.) and in contexts where connectivity can be limited.

## Key features

The <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7X4ro7VO_y8" target="_blank">platform</a> is structured around three main pillars:

- **Map Catalogue**: An online library gathering over 3,000 static maps (reference, logistics, security, or epidemiological analysis) ready for viewing or downloading.
- **GeoApps Portal**: Centralized access to web mapping applications with advanced business features (epidemiology, security, logistics, etc.).
- **Activation Portal**: A "ticketing"-inspired system allowing field teams to easily request GIS support from the experts in the GIS unit.

## Technical Implementation

- **Architecture & Administration**: Deployment of a complex hybrid infrastructure (Linux/Windows) managing the cohabitation between **Symfony** web services and the proprietary **ArcGIS Enterprise** ecosystem (Server/Portal).
- **Data Governance**: Implementation of a centralized spatial database shared by **cartographers** (desktop editing) and **developers** (API consumption), ensuring a single source of truth.
- **ETL Pipelines & Quality Control**: Automation of field data cleaning and homogenization via **FME Server** to ensure the reliability of distributed humanitarian indicators.
- **Application Ecosystem**: Use of Symfony as a secure "parent" application, orchestrating authentication and the hosting of multiple **Micro-Frontends (SPAs)** developed by different teams.
- **Security & Cataloging**: Implementation of Role-Based Access Control (**RBAC**) to protect sensitive data and a high-performance indexing engine for the map and resource inventory.
- **Hybrid Development**: Technical balance between "No-code" solutions (Esri Experience Builder) for rapid deployment and custom development (Maplibre, Mapbox, Leaflet) for complex business tools.
